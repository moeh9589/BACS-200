DISCS

Everything you need to know about discs

Hover over the image to zoom(if zoom does not work, refresh the page)

----------------------------

Every disc is different. Every disc has a different shape and a different weight. Finding the right disc is a matter of personal preference as every disc will behave differently. It might not seem like much, but the little differences in shape and weight can cause a disc to go a completely different way. Each disc has 4 numbers on it(or they should). These 4 numbers correspond to speed, glide, turn and fade. More detail on those will be down below.

Hover over the image to shake

----------------------------

Speed

The first number on each disc corresponds ot its speed. This number ranges from 1-14. The higher the speed, the easier it is to make the disc fly faster. This does not necessarily mean that you should go out and buy every disc that has a 14. This is because some discs with high speeds are harder to make them do what you want. A disc with higher speeds needs to be thrown with the rightt arm speed to put the right RPM on the disc. As for beginners, discs with speeds from 7-10 are easy to throw and make for good starter discs.

----------------------------

Glide

The second number on each disc corresponds ot its glide. It ranges from 1 to 7. This number represents a disc's ability to remain in the air. A disc with a higher glide number, like a 6, will stay in the air easier than a disc with a 2. A disc with a glide of 2 would fight more to turn over so it is angled toward the ground.

----------------------------

Turn

The third number on each disc corresponds ot its turn. It ranges from -5 to 1 When a disc is thrown, it rotates. When the disc is rotating while moving forward, one side of the disc is moving with the air flow, while the other side of the disc is moving against the air flow. This is what causes the magnus effect. Thus, the disc will have a natural motion. The turn number describes the discs tendency to resist this natural motion. If the turn is -5, and the disc is thrown right-handed and back-handed, then the disc will resist its natural motion which is to the left. This will cause the disc to turn right earlier in its flight. A disc with a 0 will not resist its natural motion.

----------------------------

Fade

The fourth and final number on each disc corresponds ot its fade. This number represents thhe discs path at the end of its flight. A disc with a fade of 0 will finish straight, while a disc with a fade of 5 will start to hook towards the discs natural flight path. So a disc with fade 5 and thrown right-handed and back-handed will hook left toward the end of its flight.
